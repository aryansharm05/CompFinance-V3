Profitability Ratios

Net Profit Margin

Net Profit Margin measures the percentage of revenue that exceeds costs. It shows how much profit a company generates from its total revenue.

Formula: Net Income √∑ Revenue

A higher net profit margin indicates that a company is more efficient at converting revenue into actual profit. Typically, a 10-20% margin is considered good, while above 20% is excellent in many industries.

Return on Equity (ROE)

Return on Equity measures how efficiently a company uses its equity to generate profits. It shows the profit generated with the money shareholders have invested.

Formula: Net Income √∑ Shareholders' Equity

ROE is a key metric for investors as it shows how effectively a company is using its equity investment. A good ROE is typically considered to be 15-20% or higher, though this varies by industry.

Return on Assets (ROA)

Return on Assets measures how efficiently a company uses its assets to generate profits.

Formula: Net Income √∑ Total Assets

A higher ROA means the company is using its assets efficiently. A ROA above 5% is generally considered good.

Liquidity Ratios

Current Ratio

Current Ratio measures a company's ability to pay short-term obligations.

Formula: Current Assets √∑ Current Liabilities

A current ratio above 1.5 is considered healthy for most businesses.

Quick Ratio

Quick Ratio measures a company's ability to meet short-term obligations with its most liquid assets.

Formula: (Current Assets - Inventory) √∑ Current Liabilities

A quick ratio above 1 is considered good, indicating the company can meet its short-term liabilities without selling inventory.

Efficiency Ratios

Asset Turnover

Asset Turnover measures how efficiently a company uses its assets to generate sales.

Formula: Sales √∑ Total Assets

A higher asset turnover ratio indicates efficient use of assets. Above 1 is generally good.

Inventory Turnover

Inventory Turnover measures how many times a company's inventory is sold and replaced over a period.

Formula: Cost of Goods Sold √∑ Inventory

A higher inventory turnover means efficient inventory management. 5-10 is typical for many industries.

Leverage Ratios

Debt to Equity

Debt to Equity measures a company's financial leverage by comparing total debt to shareholders' equity.

Formula: Total Liabilities √∑ Shareholders' Equity

A lower debt to equity ratio is generally better. A ratio below 2 is considered healthy for most businesses.
